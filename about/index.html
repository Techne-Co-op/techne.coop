Section one The cooperative

The cooperative

Techne is the studio name of RegenHub, LCA — a Colorado Limited Cooperative Association. Filed February 6, 2026. Public benefit: cultivating scenius.

Scenius is a word Brian Eno coined to name what he observed in places where exceptional work concentrates: not individual brilliance, but the collective intelligence of a scene. The genius of conditions. What arises when talented people share a commons, exchange ideas in real time, and hold each other to a higher standard of craft. The scene produces more than its members could alone.

That is what RegenHub is organized to cultivate. Not the output of any one person, but the conditions from which good work keeps emerging.

Section two The name

The name

Techne (τεχνη) is the Greek word for craft, art, skill — the knowledge that lives in the hand as much as the mind. The ancient Greeks distinguished it from episteme, abstract knowing, the kind you can write in a book. Techne is something else: the intelligence of making. You acquire it by doing, not by reading.

A surgeon operates by techne. A carpenter joints wood by techne. A programmer who can feel when a design is right, before they can say why — that is techne. It is knowing that cannot be fully separated from the act of making.

A cooperative named Techne is a claim: that the work matters, that craft is a form of thinking, that making and knowing are not separate activities. The studio exists to practice this — to build things that are well-made, and to learn from making them.

Section three How it works

How it works

RegenHub is a cooperative, which means it is owned and governed by its members — not by outside shareholders. There is no external investor to satisfy, no exit to optimize for. Surplus is reinvested in the work and in the community, not extracted.

Decisions are made collectively, through a board elected by members. Each member has a vote. The cooperative's surplus is allocated as patronage — proportional to each member's participation — rather than flowing to whoever put in capital first.

The public benefit is formally stated: cultivating scenius. That is not a slogan. It is a legal commitment, built into the entity's structure, that the cooperative exists to serve something beyond itself.

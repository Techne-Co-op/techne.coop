RegenHub, LCA · Colorado Public Benefit Limited Cooperative Association

Participation Levels

RegenHub operates a four-class membership structure across two tracks. The access track is open enrollment — community participants and co-working members who use the hub. The patron track is by invitation — cooperative members with voting rights, capital accounts, and Schedule K-1 tax reporting. A conversion pathway connects the two tracks.

Patronage Conversion Pathway
Co-working → Cooperative Member
A Co-working Member (Class Two) who reaches a board-established patronage threshold may be invited to apply for Cooperative Member (Class One) status. Upon Board acceptance, the member executes the Membership Agreement, pays the $100 one-time share, and transitions fully to the patron track. Patronage credits earned as a Co-working Member inform the invitation — the threshold and credit methodology are set by board resolution and specified in Schedule A.
Class Two — Co-working Member → patronage threshold met → Board invitation → MA executed + $100 share → Class One — Cooperative Member

Rights & Privileges
  • One vote — all cooperative governance matters
  • Patronage allocation proportional to participation
  • Capital account — grows with retained allocations
  • Annual Schedule K-1 (distributive share, IRC §702)
  • Tax Distribution — Cooperative covers estimated K-1 tax liability (adopted June 24, 2026)
  • Full financial information rights
  • Right to inspect books and records
  • Participation in surplus distribution
Admission
By invitation only. Board will not admit without approximately 90 days of established relationship (or such other period as the Board sets by resolution). Board retains discretion to admit or decline.
Withdrawal
14-day written notice. Settlement at positive capital account balance — not par value — within 90-day target, 3-year outer limit. Obligations under IP, confidentiality, and indemnification survive.

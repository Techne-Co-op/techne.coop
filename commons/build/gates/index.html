The Gate Book

A gate is a human act: people demonstrate the capability live, say the words, and record the event. Nothing here executes; this page is the score. Each gate carries its condition, its demonstration script drawn sentence by sentence from PRD v0.3 §4, the attestation to speak, and the procedure that writes it into the record.

G-B · The Belong gate anticipated

conditionB-02, B-04, B-05 verified (they are, 2026-07-12). An organizer and a non-organizer member demonstrate every Belong sentence live, together, at the August 14 gathering. Two parties, because belonging is not self-certified.

The cast: one organizer (any steward or director) and one newcomer, a person who is not an organizer and has never touched the system. The newcomer drives; the organizer witnesses and countersigns. Each beat begins with the sentence being proven, spoken aloud.

01
newcomer
“A member can complete onboarding without a meeting.” On their own device, the newcomer opens techne.coop/commons/join and submits the application with their real name and address. Nobody helps. observe: the received panel; a membership.applied event lands in the record; the steward notice arrives if the X-02 rail is wired
02
newcomer
“A member can read every agreement that binds them, with version and standing marked.” After sign-in by magic link, the newcomer opens the agreements shelf and reads the bylaws entry aloud: title, version, effective date, and their own unsigned chip. observe: version and standing marked; nothing shown that does not bind them
03
newcomer
“A member can sign the membership agreement and see the signature recorded.” The newcomer signs on the shelf and reads the confirmation panel aloud: their name, the instrument, the version, the binding statement. observe: the signature row and its signature.signed event, atomic per 0003
04
newcomer
“A member can appear in the member directory with a profile they control.” The newcomer finds themselves in the directory, edits their display name, refreshes, and shows the change held. observe: self-edit works; no other row is editable
05
organizer
Countersign. The organizer confirms each sentence was demonstrated by someone with no standing to fake it, and both speak the attestation.

The probe matrix already proves the policies behave as written; this pass proves they cite what still stands. Align the legal index and the instrument on one mark. Then attest.

Recording an attestation

One procedure for every gate, the G0 pattern. The attesting human records the event; the ledger flips by commit; the roadmap follows the ledger.

-- 1 · the event, inserted by the attesting agent (an overseer act)
insert into events (occurred_at, actor_agent_id, kind, payload)
values (
  now(),
  '<attesting agent id>',
  'gate.attested',
  jsonb_build_object(
    'gate', '<G-B | G-G | G-F | G-S | G-R>',
    'attestation', '<the words as spoken>',
    'witnesses', jsonb_build_array('<agent id>', '<agent id>')
  )
);

-- 2 · the ledger: status flips to attested with a resolution naming
--     the attesting agent, the date, and the event id. By commit.

-- 3 · the roadmap syncs to the ledger, and the next train opens.
why gates are people

Every suite in CI proves the machine agrees with itself. A gate proves the cooperative agrees with the machine. That second agreement cannot be automated, because it is the moment responsibility changes hands, and responsibility never transfers to an instrument.

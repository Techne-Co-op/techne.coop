The Commonplace
Book

The cooperative's shared record — named after the centuries-old practice of keeping what matters, organized for use, in one place everyone can read.

What it is

Scholars and craftspeople have kept commonplace books for centuries: not an archive, but a working tool. You copy in what governs your practice, what you've agreed to, what has happened and what it led to. The entries are organized for use rather than display. When someone needs to understand the whole, they open the book.

RegenHub's Commonplace Book is that practice carried into cooperative life. It holds the authoritative state of the cooperative: who the members are, what agreements bind them, what events have taken place, and what each person's share of the work and the results amounts to. Every figure can be traced to the events that produced it. Every rule can be traced to the governing document that established it. Nothing is hidden behind a decision no one can follow back to its source.

The book is not an app for doing the work. It is the layer that remembers what has been done — so that the next question has an honest answer, and the next member doesn't have to ask.

What it holds

Slice 01
Belong
A member can

Read every agreement that binds them, with version and standing marked. Sign the membership agreement and see the signature recorded. Appear in the member directory with a profile they control. Complete onboarding without a meeting.

Slice 02
Gather
A member can

See upcoming events and sessions at the studio. Register, and cancel, without asking anyone. Have attendance counted where the record counts it. Host an event under a named agreement or policy.

Anticipated · Aug 14, 2026
Slice 03
Find one another
A member can

Browse open offers and invitations — to play, practice, and work. Post their own offer or need, and close it when it resolves. See what an invitation led to, when the parties choose to record it.

Following Belong
Slice 04
See your share
A member can

See their contribution history, itemized. See their capital account as a fold over that history. Follow any figure to the events and the policy that produced it. Balances computed, never stored as editable numbers.

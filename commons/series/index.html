§1

What this series is

In an agent-driven build, these artifacts are not documentation of the work. They are the medium of it. A build agent's working context is assembled from exactly these documents, so each one is versioned in the repository, citable by section, and honest about its own status. An ambiguity here is an ambiguity multiplied across every packet of work downstream.

This series is a refactor, not a greenfield. An estate already exists at the commons repository: a seventy-milestone ledger organized into waves, ten recorded decisions, an operating contract for agents, a validator that enforces dependency order, a schema reference, a probe matrix, gate definitions, and live instruments. Much of that estate was shaped around a grant that no longer exists, and the design discipline it carried has been retired as a requirement. The mechanics, however, were sound. Each entry in this series therefore carries a lineage line naming what it supersedes, what it salvages, and what it retires, so that nothing is erased and nothing is quietly carried forward either.

Two vocabularies bind the whole set. The status marks (Filed, Ratified, Drafted, Anticipated, Open) apply to every claim in every artifact. And the four slices named on the public page (Belong, Gather, Find one another, See your share) are the shared shape of the build, so that the public promise and the work plan use one language. The series includes itself: SER is the first entry in its own register.

§2

How artifacts cite, version, and block

Because there are no time estimates, readiness does the scheduling. A packet of work is ready when everything it cites is settled enough, and a blocked packet wears the chip of the item that blocks it, by name.

The citation grammar is code, version, section: IM v0.1 §3 is a complete address. The section addressing scheme already decided in the estate (D7) carries forward unchanged. Supersession is always explicit: a new version names what it replaces in its preamble, and the replaced document remains in the record as history. Nothing in this series is deleted; things are succeeded.

Three further disciplines carry from the estate and from board practice. First, only humans record decisions; agents may improve a deliberation brief but never resolve it. Second, the vocabulary quarantine holds: Subchapter K grammar throughout (distributive share, capital account, allocation), with the retired cooperative-tax vocabulary kept out of every artifact. Third, provenance is marked conservatively: agent-authored work is labeled as such, and simulated or illustrative entries write nothing back to the live record.

§5

The public door

The landing page is a series member, not an afterthought, because it is the artifact where the refactor's positioning becomes a public promise.

Its rule is simple: the page may claim only what a series artifact holds. The current index at the commons address leads with the build (milestones, waves, decision registers); the replacement leads with the system, in the voice of the v0.1 introduction, and lets the instruments recede to a quiet strip. Whether one page serves both the cooperative's public address and the repository's front door, or the two addresses carry different registers of the same page, is an open design choice that belongs with Todd, as does the working name itself.

naming · The Common Record is proposed, not adopted · decision with Todd Open
§6

What the series retires, and what it leaves out

A covering document earns its keep by naming its edges.

Retired with the grant: the Hub Operations PRD as a governing specification, the KPI fixture pack, the liaison interface decision, and the KPI-gated wave. Their salvageable parts are named in the register above; the documents themselves remain in the record as history. The eight remaining recorded decisions carry forward unless a slice reopens one, and the two ratification items stay open where they belong, with the members, gated to August 14.

Left out of the series, deliberately: the bylaws, membership agreement, and patronage policy, which are upstream dependencies owned by the board and counsel rather than build artifacts, though AM and PRD consume them. The dispatch cadence, which documents the build publicly but does not govern it. And member-facing content, which is an output of the system rather than an input to it.

the working agreement of the series

Nothing erased, everything succeeded. Every claim wears a mark. Dependency is the schedule. Agents draft and build; people decide. And the public page never says more than the shelf can back.

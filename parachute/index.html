How it works

One vault, two publishing modes

Every entry you write sits in your private vault first. You choose what to make visible to your peer group — and when. Nothing is public by default.

Private
Yours alone
Drafts, reflections, unfinished thinking. Your individual layer. Only you can read it.
Peer group
Held in common
Settled entries posted to the group's shared daybook. Visible to all members of your group.

The Daybook as surface

The Daybook is not a note app. It's a book of original entry — a cooperative instrument for recording work, time, and value as it happens, in the colour of the hour it occurs.

Built on the Parachute vault protocol, it renders your notes in the seasonal rhythm of the day: each hour has a colour, each entry settles into the bloom of the day's work.

The Daybook

A surface shaped around how cooperative work actually moves

Each entry carries a type — reflection, contribution, exchange, clearing — and a time. The group's shared daybook accumulates these into a bloom: a visualization of what the collective has made public, by hour, by kind.

Private entries stay in your vault, never visible in the shared bloom, until you choose to post them. The act of posting is the act of contributing to the common record.

Cloud credits — pay only for what you use

Parachute Cloud runs on a service credit model. One cloud credit covers ten cents of compute, storage, and vault operations. Credits never expire. There's no subscription, no seat pricing, no monthly minimum beyond your opening balance.

The $3 opening balance is thirty credits — enough to use the Daybook actively for a month, try the API, and understand your own usage before deciding how much to add.

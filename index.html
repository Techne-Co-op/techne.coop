01A year in the making

We took our time to build this right.

Techne did not appear overnight. It grew out of a year of weekly conversations among people already sharing space and work, who decided to make their cooperation real and legible.

01
Aug 2025

A question worth making real

A group already sharing a space asked whether to become a cooperative in earnest, owned by the people who use it.

02
Autumn 2025

Soil, not a firm

We chose to be the ground that ventures grow from, and to reinvest what we earn into the community rather than extract it.

03
Late 2025

The careful legal work

We worked with counsel, committed to public benefit in writing, and chose the cooperative form that fits how we already work.

04
Feb 6, 2026

A real vessel

We filed as a Colorado public-benefit cooperative, operating as Techne. A legal home for the work, and for its members.

02What you would be joining

A studio, a practice, and a commons.

Membership is not a subscription to a product. It is a stake in a shared workshop and in the tools that come out of it.

A studio and a space

A coworking studio in downtown Boulder. A real place to do the work, alongside people who care about how it gets made.

Programs that teach by building

Learning that happens by making things, not just watching. A way to grow skills with others, and to bring your own work into the room.

Tools held in common

We build open tools for ourselves first, then share them. The infrastructure stays a commons, not a product locked behind a paywall.

A share in the whole

Members are owners. Decisions are made one member, one vote, and surplus flows back into the community rather than out to shareholders.

03Three ways in

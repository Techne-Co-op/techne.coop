Techne The work

How Techne works.

A cooperative approach to making: scoped, legible, held in common.

Section one Collective work

Collective work

Techne operates as a collective — work is proposed, claimed, and executed through a shared coordination process. A sprint is a bounded unit of work: scoped, time-limited, reviewed. No single person holds the whole picture; the work is legible to everyone.

This is not bureaucracy for its own sake. Legibility is what makes collective work possible. When a sprint is finished, its scope, its outcome, and its author are all on the record. That record is how a cooperative distributes patronage fairly, and how new members can find their footing quickly.

01

Discovery

A need or opportunity is surfaced. Anyone can propose; the collective decides what is worth pursuing.

02

Proposal

A sprint is written: scope, timeline, and expected output. Proposals are public within the cooperative.

03

Negotiation

Members review, refine, and approve. A sprint is not started until its shape is agreed.

04

Execution

The sprint runs. Work is done in the open. Questions surface early rather than late.

05

Synthesis

The work is reviewed and completed. Outcomes are recorded. The record becomes the basis for patronage.

Section two The commons

The commons

We build and maintain a commons — shared infrastructure, shared instruments, shared knowledge. The commons is not a product; it is the condition that makes individual work possible. What gets built here is held by the cooperative, not by any one person.

A commons requires care. It does not maintain itself. Part of what it means to be a member of Techne is a commitment to that care: to fix what breaks, to document what you know, to leave things better than you found them.

This is what distinguishes a cooperative from a platform. A platform extracts value from its participants; a cooperative holds value for them. The commons is the concrete form that holding takes.

Section three Instruments

Instruments

We build tools we use ourselves. The cooperative's instruments — for coordination, accounting, governance, and communication — are built in-house and used in practice. A tool that is not used is not finished.

This is the techne of it: craft proven by use. There is no separation between the people who design the tool and the people who depend on it. The feedback is immediate and the stakes are real.

Building for your own use also sets a different standard. You cannot ship something broken and move on. You are the one who has to live with it. This is a form of accountability that no specification document can replicate.

Projects will be listed here as the cooperative's work matures.
